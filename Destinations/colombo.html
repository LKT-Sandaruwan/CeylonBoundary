Introduction and History

Colombo is the largest city and commercial capital of Sri Lanka, located on the country‚Äôs west coast. Its strategic harbour has made it a key port city for centuries, trading with Indian, Arab, Chinese, and European merchants. Over time, colonial powers (Portuguese, Dutch, British) built infrastructure, governmental buildings, fortifications, and road networks; modern Colombo today blends colonial heritage with contemporary urban development.

Best Time to Visit

The best months are December to March, when rainfall is lower and weather is relatively dry ‚Äî good for walking and outdoor exploration. The monsoon (May to September) and inter-monsoon periods bring heavier rains, making outdoor activity less predictable.

Important Things to Note When Visiting

  • Attire: Lightweight, breathable clothes are ideal. When visiting religious sites (temples, mosques), dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees).
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes are recommended because of uneven pavements, steps, and walking around city attractions.
  • Sun & Hydration: Use sunscreen, hats, sunglasses, and carry water ‚Äî the tropical sun can be strong.
  • Traffic & Transport: Colombo traffic can be congested; plan extra travel time, use public transport, rideshares, or tuk-tuks for short hops.
  • Safety: Standard urban vigilance applies ‚Äî keep belongings safe, avoid poorly lit lanes at night, use reliable transport.
  • Weather Preparedness: Be ready for sudden rain showers, carry a light raincoat or umbrella.
