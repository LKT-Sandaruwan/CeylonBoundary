Unawatuna

Serene Beach Town with Rich History and Biodiversity

Introduction and History

Unawatuna, located approximately 5 km southeast of Galle, is a coastal town renowned for its crescent-shaped beach, coral reefs, and vibrant marine life. Historically, it played a role during the colonial period, with the Dutch establishing settlements in the area. The town also holds mythological significance, with legends linking it to the Ramayana epic. Over the years, Unawatuna has evolved into a popular tourist destination, balancing its rich heritage with modern amenities.

Best Time to Visit

The optimal time to visit Unawatuna is between November and April, during the dry season. During these months, the sea is calm, making it ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and diving. The weather is generally sunny, with minimal rainfall, providing perfect conditions for beach activities and exploration.

Important Travel Notes

  • Attire: Light, breathable clothing is recommended due to the warm coastal climate. Swimwear is acceptable on the beach, but modest attire is advisable when visiting religious sites.
  • Safety Equipment: Light, breathable clothing is recommended due to the warm coastal climate. Swimwear is acceptable on the beach, but modest attire is advisable when visiting religious sites.
  • Health Precautions: Apply sunscreen regularly to protect against sunburn. Insect repellent is advisable, especially during the evenings, to ward off mosquitoes.
  • Footwear: Comfortable sandals or flip-flops are suitable for the beach, while sturdy shoes are recommended for exploring nearby attractions like Rumassala Hill.
  • Snorkeling/Diving Gear: While equipment can be rented locally, bringing personal gear ensures better hygiene and fit.
  • Hydration: Carry water bottles to stay hydrated during your visit.
