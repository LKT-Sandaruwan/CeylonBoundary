The Ancient Capital of Sri Lanka

Introduction and History

Polonnaruwa, located in the North Central Province of Sri Lanka, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its well-preserved ruins of ancient Sri Lankan civilization. Serving as the second capital of Sri Lanka after the destruction of Anuradhapura in 993 AD, Polonnaruwa thrived under the reign of King Parakramabahu I in the 12th century. The city showcases a blend of Buddhist and Hindu architectural structures, including stupas, temples, statues, and irrigation systems that highlight the advanced engineering skills of the time.

Important Travel Notes

  • Attire: Wear light, breathable clothing suitable for warm weather.
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes are advisable, as exploring the archaeological sites involves substantial walking.
  • Sun Protection: Use sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses to protect against sun exposure.
  • Hydration: Carry water bottles to stay hydrated during your visit.
  • Cultural Sensitivity: When visiting religious sites, dress modestly, covering shoulders and knees.
