Introduction and History

Yala National Park is Sri Lanka‚Äôs most visited national park, known for its rich biodiversity ‚Äî especially its leopard population. The park covers approximately 979 km¬≤ (about 378 square miles) and includes several ecosystems: monsoon forests, scrub, wetlands, grasslands, and coastline. Yala was designated a wildlife sanctuary in 1900 and later established as a national park in 1938. It is divided into multiple blocks; two of these are commonly open to visitors (e.g. Block I / Katagamuwa & Palatupana).

Best Time to Visit

The dry season from June to September is ideal for wildlife viewing, as animals congregate near water sources. Early morning and late afternoon safaris are recommended for better lighting and more active wildlife. The park is generally open from 6:00 am to 6:00 pm.

Important Travel Notes

  • Clothing: Wear neutral-colored, lightweight clothing to blend in with surroundings and avoid startling animals.
  • Footwear: Closed, comfortable shoes suitable for a dusty jeep safari.
  • Sun & Hydration: Bring sunscreen, a brimmed hat, sunglasses, and plenty of water.
  • Insect Protection: Use insect repellent, especially for the wetter / marshy zones.
  • Guides & Timing: Always go with an authorized safari guide; start early in the morning or later in the afternoon to avoid midday heat.
  • Respect Wildlife: Do not exit the vehicle; maintain distance; avoid flash photography.
  • Camera Gear: Bring a telephoto lens or zoom for wildlife photography.
  • Park Blocks & Access: Confirm which block is open on your day (some blocks may be closed for maintenance).
