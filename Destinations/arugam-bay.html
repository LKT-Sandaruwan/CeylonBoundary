Arugam Bay

Surfing Paradise and Coastal Retreat

Introduction and History

Arugam Bay, located on Sri Lanka's southeast coast in the Ampara District, is renowned for its pristine beaches and world-class surf breaks. Historically a modest fishing village, it has transformed into a vibrant tourist destination, attracting surfers and beach enthusiasts worldwide. The area was significantly impacted by the 2004 tsunami but has since rebuilt and flourished as a hub for tourism.

Best Time to Visit

The optimal time to visit Arugam Bay is between May and September, coinciding with the dry season on the east coast. During these months, the weather is sunny with minimal rainfall, and the ocean conditions are ideal for surfing and other water activities.

Important Travel Notes

  • Attire: Lightweight, breathable clothing is recommended due to the warm climate. Swimwear is appropriate for the beach, but modest attire is advisable when visiting religious sites.
  • Safety Equipment: When engaging in water activities like surfing or snorkeling, ensure you use appropriate safety gear and follow local guidelines.
  • Health Precautions: Apply sunscreen regularly to protect against sunburn. Insect repellent is advisable, especially during the evenings, to ward off mosquitoes.
  • Footwear: Comfortable sandals or flip-flops are suitable for the beach, while sturdy shoes are recommended for exploring nearby attractions.
  • Hydration: Carry water bottles to stay hydrated during your visit.
  • Insect Protection: Use mosquito repellent containing DEET, wear long sleeves and pants during dawn and dusk, and sleep under mosquito nets to prevent bites.
