Introduction and History

Udawalawe National Park was officially declared on 30 June 1972 to protect wildlife displaced by the construction of the Udawalawe Reservoir on the Walawe River. Best Time to Visit

The dry season (May to September) is considered ideal for wildlife viewing, as animals gather near water sources. The park remains open year-round, unlike some parks that close during monsoon seasons. Early morning and late afternoon safaris offer the best lighting and most active animal behavior.

Important Things to Note When Visiting

  • Attire: Neutral, earth-tone clothing helps blend with surroundings; avoid bright colors.
  • Footwear: Closed, sturdy shoes are best, given rough terrain and dusty tracks.
  • Sun & Hydration: Bring sunscreen, a hat, sunglasses, and sufficient water.
  • Insect Protection: Use insect repellent, especially near marshes or moist areas.
  • Safari Etiquette: Stay inside the vehicle, avoid loud noises, and follow guide instructions.
  • Equipment: A telephoto lens is helpful for wildlife photography.
  • Rules: No littering, feeding animals, or leaving the vehicle.
